Enterprise AI Security Best Practices for 2026

Published April 2026 — Security Research Team

As enterprises accelerate adoption of generative AI tools, the security landscape has shifted dramatically. Organizations now face a dual challenge: enabling productivity while preventing sensitive data from leaking through AI-powered interfaces.

Key Insight: Browser extensions designed to enforce DLP policies on GenAI platforms have become a critical control point. But what happens when the security tool itself becomes the attack surface?

This article explores the architectural patterns that make browser-based DLP solutions vulnerable to exploitation from any webpage, and demonstrates why origin validation is non-negotiable for extension security.

Topics covered:

  • Content script message relay patterns and their risks
  • The externally_connectable wildcard anti-pattern
  • How postMessage bridges expose privileged extension APIs
  • Real-world attack chains: from page visit to total product defeat
